home/about

we don't have a legacy. we have opinions.

indéva studio is an architecture-led interior design firm based in new delhi. we work from structure, proportion, and movement — never from surface styling. what gets built is the only truth we recognise.

the studio

founded to challenge how interiors are designed and delivered.

too many projects in india are built on surface aesthetics, unrealistic promises, and a lack of accountability once execution begins. moodboards replace drawings. renders replace decisions. the contractor is treated as a vendor rather than a collaborator. the result is rooms that photograph well and inhabit poorly.

indéva studio operates differently. our work is rooted in architecture before decoration. every choice — from spatial planning to door handles — is examined for whether it earns its place. we speak openly about budgets, timelines, and constraints. we do not disappear after the drawing phase. we are on site until the project is finished as intended.

we are not building a brand around a founder's celebrity. we are building a studio around a process — one that produces homes and spaces that perform in real life and age with their occupants.

the founder

arpit saini.

arpit saini is the founder and ceo of indéva studio. he established the firm to bring architectural rigour to a category that had drifted into pure decoration — to build a studio that respects clients with honest conversations about budget, scope, and timeline, and treats every drawing as an execution document, not marketing material.

his approach is to interrogate every brief before responding to it. who lives here. how do they wake up. how do they entertain. where does the light arrive. what gets used daily. what gets used once a year. interiors are not decorated; they are designed for a life. that conviction is reflected in every project the studio takes on.

"we don't chase trends or design for applause. we believe good design should perform well in real life and age with time. that requires conviction, difficult conversations, and accountability from start to finish."
how we think

three principles we work by.

pillar 01

human-centricity first

design is a service to the soul, not just an aesthetic exercise. we prioritise habitability before selecting a colour palette. we map morning light, the flow of conversation, and the physical comfort of a seated guest. a chair is only beautiful if it invites you to stay.

pillar 02

the invisible detail

true luxury is felt, not seen. we obsess over the elements you don't notice until they're missing — the silent glide of a drawer, the tactile weight of a door handle, the acoustic warmth of a well-layered room. invisible engineering is the foundation that lets the aesthetic feel effortless.

pillar 03

the art of the mix

we reject the total look of a single era or brand. we pair sleek modern industrialism with the warmth of an antique. this juxtaposition creates a dialogue that prevents a space from feeling dated and allows it to remain timeless through its eclectic soul.

how we work

before aesthetics, we establish clarity.

our process moves in four stages, with no shortcut between them. each stage produces a deliverable the client signs off before the next begins.

01 / alignment

brief, budget & intent set

we map who lives here, how, and why. we agree on budget, scope, and constraints in writing.

02 / design

spatial logic, concept, visualisation

spatial planning, material strategy, lighting, and concept visualisation. drawings, not vibes.

03 / execution

drawings, site control, coordination

working drawings issued to contractors. site supervision, vendor management, daily coordination.

04 / handover

final checks & flawless delivery

punch list, snagging, final walk-through. handover document. one-year defect liability.

where we work

new delhi, and across india.

our studio is based in new delhi. we work pan-india, with active markets across delhi, gurgaon, and noida, with completed projects in udaipur, dehradun, and other indian cities.

for clients outside the ncr, we travel for site visits at agreed milestones and maintain daily coordination with on-ground execution teams. distance is a logistics problem, not a design constraint.
