designing a luxury villa in india: what separates the extraordinary from the expensive

november 2023 · 9 min read
india's finest villas share one quality that no budget can buy: spatial intelligence. we examine what makes a villa truly luxurious — from site planning through to the last door handle.

the luxury villa is the most complex residential brief in interior design. it must function as a private home — accommodating the rhythms and needs of a specific family — while also serving as a site of hospitality, a display of taste and achievement, and, often, a long-term investment asset. the competing demands of these functions require spatial intelligence of the highest order. a villa that achieves only one of these goals — the home that is perfect for its occupants but intimidating to guests; the villa that photographs magnificently but lives poorly — has failed in its fundamental brief.

site planning: the foundation everything else rests on

the planning of a villa begins not with the building but with the site. the orientation of the main living spaces relative to the sun, the positioning of the primary arrival sequence, the relationship between indoor and outdoor spaces, and the management of privacy from adjacent properties and roads — these decisions, made at the site planning stage, determine every spatial quality of the finished villa. a villa that has been incorrectly oriented — living spaces facing west in a hot climate, for example — cannot be corrected by interior design. it can only be mitigated, imperfectly and expensively.

"spatial intelligence in a villa cannot be purchased at specification stage. it must be present at site planning stage, or it will be absent throughout."

the arrival sequence: first impressions as design

the experience of arriving at a villa — from the gate through to the primary reception space — is one of the most important design sequences in the project. it establishes the emotional register of the entire home, it communicates the quality and intelligence of the design to guests before they have crossed the threshold, and it performs the psychological transition from public to private that makes a home feel like a sanctuary. great arrival sequences are rare because they require spatial generosity — land that could be built on, budget that could be spent on interiors — and because they don't photograph easily. they must be experienced.

material quality at villa scale

material specification at villa scale is different from apartment specification in one fundamental way: scale reveals quality. a material that looks acceptable at sample size and acceptable in a small room looks different across 200 square metres of flooring, or across a 6-metre ceiling. the veining in natural stone must be considered as a composition at room scale, not just as a texture at sample scale. the colour consistency of handmade tiles must be considered across an entire installation, not just a single piece. and the quality of joinery and finish must withstand the scrutiny that scale and light inevitably produce in large spaces.
