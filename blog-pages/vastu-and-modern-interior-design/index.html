vastu and modern interior design: how to honour both without compromising either

september 2023 · 9 min read
vastu shastra and contemporary design are not in conflict — if you understand what each is actually asking for. here is how india's best designers reconcile ancient principles with modern living.

vastu shastra — the ancient indian system of spatial organisation — is present in a significant proportion of residential design briefs across india. for some clients, it is a deeply held spiritual and cultural commitment. for others, it is a family expectation that they respect without necessarily subscribing to. for others still, it is a box they would like to check without compromising their design aspirations. how a designer responds to this requirement reveals a great deal about their cultural intelligence and their design maturity.

what vastu actually prescribes

vastu shastra is a system of spatial organisation based on the relationship between the built environment and cardinal directions, solar movement, and the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space). its primary prescriptions concern the placement of key rooms and functional areas: the northeast zone is associated with water and purity, making it appropriate for puja rooms, meditation spaces, and bathrooms; the southeast is associated with fire, making it appropriate for kitchens; the northwest is associated with air, making it appropriate for guest bedrooms; and the southwest is associated with earth and stability, making it appropriate for the master bedroom. the entrance is ideally in the north, northeast, or east, to receive morning light and its associated energy.

"vastu and good design share more than they conflict. both are asking the same fundamental question: what is the right relationship between this space and the people who inhabit it?"

where vastu and good design align

the most remarkable aspect of vastu for a designer who understands both systems is how frequently good vastu and good contemporary design produce identical prescriptions. a northeast kitchen is inauspicious in vastu — and it is also, in most site configurations, poorly oriented for natural ventilation and morning light. a southwest master bedroom is auspicious — and it is also, typically, the quietest and most private location in the plan, appropriate for sleeping. the prescriptions of vastu are not arbitrary; they encode centuries of observed relationships between spatial orientation and human wellbeing. modern environmental psychology is, in many respects, arriving at the same conclusions through a different methodology.

when genuine conflicts arise

genuine conflicts between vastu principles and good contemporary design most commonly arise when the site itself is oriented in a direction that makes vastu compliance and spatial quality mutually exclusive. in these cases, we take the following approach: prioritise the spatial decisions that have the greatest impact on how the home functions and feels; apply vastu principles where they can be implemented without spatial compromise; and where a genuine conflict exists, explain it clearly to the client so they can make an informed decision rather than a constrained one.
