sustainable interior design: building beautiful spaces responsibly

june 2024 · 8 min read
sustainability in interiors is not a trend — it is a responsibility. here's how thoughtful material choices, local sourcing, and considered design create spaces that are better for everyone.

sustainable interior design has been, for most of its history as a concept, treated as an aesthetic category — the presence of plants, natural materials, and a vaguely organic colour palette. this is a misunderstanding of what sustainability in design actually means. genuine sustainable design is not a look. it is a set of decisions — about materials, supply chains, longevity, and the embedded carbon of every object that enters a space — that cumulatively reduce the environmental impact of a project without compromising its quality.

material provenance: the most consequential decision

the single most sustainable design decision you can make is to source materials locally. a natural stone quarried in rajasthan and transported 500 kilometres to delhi has a fraction of the carbon footprint of an imported italian marble. a solid timber piece made by a craftsman in moradabad has a fraction of the embedded energy of a piece manufactured in europe and shipped across two continents. local sourcing also supports regional craft economies — which are, in india, among the most sustainable forms of production in the world.

"the most sustainable material is the one that lasts the longest. choose quality once rather than replacing mediocrity repeatedly."

longevity as sustainability

the most sustainable interior is one that doesn't need to be redesigned in ten years. this seems obvious but is consistently overlooked. when we choose materials based on their durability rather than their immediate cost, when we design spaces that can absorb change without requiring structural intervention, and when we specify furniture that is built to last decades rather than years, we are making a sustainability decision that dwarfs any individual material choice in its long-term impact. the interior that needs complete replacement every decade generates ten times the material waste of the interior designed to last a lifetime.

voc emissions and indoor air quality

indoor air quality is a sustainability issue that directly affects the health of the people who inhabit a space. conventional paints, adhesives, sealants, and composite wood products release volatile organic compounds that contribute to poor indoor air quality. in india, where homes are frequently well-sealed against heat and dust, the concentration of these compounds can be significant. specifying low-voc paints, natural adhesives, and solid wood rather than mdf or plywood wherever possible is both a sustainability decision and a health one.
