how much does interior design cost in india? a complete 2025 breakdown

december 2023 · 10 min read
from per-square-foot rates to full turnkey fees, this is the most transparent breakdown of interior design pricing in india — what you get at every level, and where the hidden costs live.

pricing transparency is one of the most significant gaps in the indian interior design industry. clients approach projects without realistic benchmarks, designers quote on terms that clients don't fully understand, and the gap between expectation and reality produces conflict and disappointment. this is a breakdown of what interior design actually costs in india in 2025 — not aspirationally, not as a marketing tool, but as an honest account of what clients can expect to pay at different levels of quality and service.

understanding the pricing structures

interior design pricing in india operates through three primary structures. the per-square-foot model charges a fixed rate per square foot of the project area — typically used by mid-market firms and package-based services. the percentage of project cost model charges a percentage of the total construction and procurement cost — typically used by established design firms. and the fixed fee model charges a predetermined fee for a defined scope of services — used by firms that can accurately estimate the time their work will take. each model has different implications for the client's incentives and the designer's accountability.

"the cheapest quote is almost always the most expensive project. the variables that get excluded from a low quote are the variables that determine quality."

what you get at different price points

at the entry level (₹800–1500 per square foot for a residential project): package-based design with limited customisation, contractor-supplied materials at standard grades, and limited design oversight during execution. at the mid-market level (₹1500–3000 per square foot): customised design with broader material options, design firm-appointed contractors, and regular site supervision. at the premium level (₹3000–6000 per square foot): fully bespoke design, premium material specifications, dedicated project management, and comprehensive execution oversight. at the luxury level (above ₹6000 per square foot): complete design and project management service, custom furniture and joinery specification, imported or premium Indian materials, and continuous on-site presence during critical execution phases.

where the hidden costs live

the costs that most frequently surprise clients are: civil and structural work (often not included in design quotations but typically the largest cost component of a renovation); electrical rewiring (old buildings frequently require complete rewiring to support contemporary load requirements, at significant cost); procurement logistics (transport, duties, and installation of specified items); and variation orders — changes made during execution that alter the agreed scope. the most important question to ask any designer before appointment is: what exactly is included and excluded from this quotation?
