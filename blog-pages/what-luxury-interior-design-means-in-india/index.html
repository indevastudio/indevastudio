what luxury interior design actually means in india

february 2025 · 7 min read
luxury is not a price point — it's a philosophy. we break down what separates genuinely luxurious interiors from expensive ones, and why the distinction matters more than ever.

there are two kinds of expensive interiors in india. the first kind is expensive because it is luxurious. the second kind is expensive because it has cost a great deal of money. these are not the same thing. the distinction matters enormously — both to the clients who are paying for design work and to the designers who are producing it.

what luxury is not

luxury is not the sum of expensive materials. it is not marble on every surface. it is not a chandelier in every room. it is not a brand name on every fixture. we have seen interiors specified entirely from top-tier european suppliers — every element individually excellent — that felt oppressive, confused, and exhausting. the materials were luxurious. the design was not.

luxury, in the context of residential interior design, is the quality of feeling exactly right. not impressive. not expensive. right. a room that is luxurious feels like it could not be any other way — like every decision was inevitable, like nothing is there that doesn't need to be there, like the space has been designed for the specific people who inhabit it.

"genuine luxury is invisible. it is the feeling that nothing could be added and nothing should be removed."

what luxury actually requires

genuine luxury in interior design requires three things that cannot be bought: time, restraint, and specificity. time — because luxury is not achieved through a fast process. it requires multiple site visits, extended material testing, careful detailing, and the patience to wait for the right solution rather than accepting a good-enough one. restraint — because luxury spaces are always edited. they contain less than they could. every element that remains has survived a process of interrogation. and specificity — because a luxury interior is bespoke to its site and its occupants in a way that means it could not exist anywhere else.

luxury in the indian context

the indian luxury market has specific characteristics that make the definition both more complex and more interesting than in european or american contexts. indian luxury clients are, in general, more knowledgeable about craftsmanship than their western counterparts — they have grown up in households with hand-embroidered textiles, hand-carved furniture, and hand-crafted objects that western luxury markets have largely lost access to. this means that the bar for material quality, finish, and craft is very high — and that genuine indian luxury draws on this tradition, not as a nostalgic gesture, but as a living standard of quality.
