farmhouse interior design ideas india: a luxury penthouse project in noida

in a bustling city like noida, it's rare to find a space that exudes the warmth and coziness of a farmhouse. but that's exactly what our client, a young couple, wanted to achieve in their penthouse apartment in sector 150, noida. with a budget of ₹40–80 lakh, they approached indéva studio to bring their vision to life.

the brief

our client's brief was clear: they wanted a farmhouse-style interior that would transport them to the countryside, without leaving the city. they showed us pictures of rustic barns, wooden beams, and vintage decor, and asked us to incorporate these elements into their penthouse design. we were given a timeline of 12 weeks to complete the project, with a budget of ₹60 lakh.

the challenge

the biggest challenge we faced was balancing the client's desire for a rustic, farmhouse-style interior with the sleek, modern aesthetic of the penthouse apartment. the space had large windows, minimal ornamentation, and a neutral color palette, which seemed at odds with the cozy, eclectic vibe of a farmhouse. we had to think creatively to incorporate the client's desired elements without compromising the overall aesthetic of the space.

design decisions

we started by selecting a few statement pieces, such as a reclaimed wood beam and a vintage metal chandelier, to anchor the space and set the tone for the rest of the design. we then chose a mix of rustic and modern materials, such as wooden floors, stone countertops, and metal accents, to add depth and visual interest to the space. to stay within budget, we opted for ₹50,000 worth of reclaimed wood for the beam, and ₹20,000 for the vintage chandelier.

  • reclaimed wood beam: ₹50,000
  • vintage metal chandelier: ₹20,000
  • wooden floors: ₹15,000
  • stone countertops: ₹30,000
  • metal accents: ₹10,000

the design solution

our design solution was to create a space that blended the warmth and coziness of a farmhouse with the sleek, modern aesthetic of the penthouse apartment. we used a combination of rustic and modern materials, and incorporated vintage and antique pieces to add character and personality to the space. we also made sure to balance the client's desire for a cozy, eclectic vibe with the need for a functional, modern living space.

the key to successful design is not just about creating a beautiful space, but also about understanding the client's needs and desires. in this case, our client wanted a farmhouse-style interior that would transport them to the countryside, without leaving the city. we had to think creatively to incorporate the client's desired elements without compromising the overall aesthetic of the space.

the result

the final result was a space that exceeded our client's expectations. the penthouse apartment was transformed into a warm, cozy, and inviting space that felt like a farmhouse in the countryside. the reclaimed wood beam and vintage metal chandelier added a touch of rustic charm, while the wooden floors and stone countertops provided a sense of warmth and sophistication. the total cost of the project came in at ₹65 lakh, which was within the client's budget of ₹40–80 lakh.
