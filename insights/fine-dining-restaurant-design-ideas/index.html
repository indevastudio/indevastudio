fine dining restaurant design ideas india: a case study

in the heart of lutyens delhi, delhi, a stunning heritage bungalow stood as a testament to the city's rich history. our client, a renowned restaurateur, had envisioned converting this beautiful property into a fine dining restaurant that would cater to the city's elite. with a budget of ₹25–50 lakh, the client approached indéva studio to bring this vision to life.

the brief

our client's primary objective was to create an elegant and sophisticated space that served high-end cuisine. the restaurant was to have a seating capacity of 50 guests, with a private dining area for special occasions. the client also emphasized the importance of preserving the heritage bungalow's original charm and character.

understanding the space

our team at indéva studio began by conducting a thorough site analysis of the heritage bungalow. we noted the existing architectural features, such as the high ceilings, wooden floors, and large windows, which would become the foundation of our design.

the challenge

one of the major challenges we faced was working within the constraints of the existing structure. the heritage bungalow's layout was not initially designed to accommodate a fine dining restaurant, and we had to navigate through the complexities of creating a functional and efficient space.

the key to overcoming this challenge was to strike a balance between preserving the original character of the bungalow and incorporating modern elements that would enhance the dining experience.

the design solution

our design team decided to adopt a minimalist approach, allowing the heritage bungalow's original features to take center stage. we allocated ₹15 lakh for the restoration of the wooden floors and ₹8 lakh for the refurbishment of the windows. the remaining ₹22 lakh was allocated for the design and construction of the restaurant's interior, including the private dining area, kitchen, and restrooms.

  • we created a sophisticated color palette, featuring muted tones and rich textures, to complement the bungalow's existing architecture.
  • the private dining area was designed to accommodate 12 guests, with a customized dining table and chairs.
  • a state-of-the-art kitchen was installed, equipped with high-end appliances and a team of skilled chefs.

selecting materials and finishes

in keeping with the client's vision, we selected materials and finishes that were both luxurious and understated. the walls were adorned with intricate moldings, and the floors were polished to a high sheen. the furniture was custom-designed to complement the bungalow's original features.

the result

after a timeline of 12 weeks, our team at indéva studio successfully completed the fine dining restaurant project. the final cost came in at ₹42 lakh, which was within the client's budget of ₹25–50 lakh. the restaurant's elegant and sophisticated atmosphere was met with rave reviews from food critics and patrons alike.
