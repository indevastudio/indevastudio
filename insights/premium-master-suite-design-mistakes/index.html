premium master suite design india: mistakes to avoid

the day a client called us mid-project, frantic about the mess their weekend retreat in KMP Expressway, Sonipat was turning out to be, was a stark reminder of the importance of careful planning in premium master suite design. with a budget of ₹8-15 lakh, the stakes were high, and the client was on the verge of losing control of their dream project.

insufficient natural lighting

one of the most common mistakes in master suite design is insufficient natural lighting. this can make the space feel dull and uninviting, and can even affect the occupants' mood and energy levels. it happens when designers fail to consider the orientation of the room and the placement of windows.

to fix this, consider the following:

  • orient the room to face the direction that receives the most natural light
  • use large windows and doors to maximize natural light entry
  • add mirrors and reflective surfaces to bounce natural light around the room

inadequate storage

inadequate storage is another common mistake in master suite design. this can lead to clutter and disorganization, making the space feel cramped and uncomfortable. it happens when designers fail to consider the clients' storage needs and provide sufficient closet and drawer space.

to fix this, consider the following:

the key to adequate storage is to provide a mix of open and closed storage options, allowing clients to choose how they want to display their belongings. this can include walk-in closets, drawer units, and open shelving.

customized storage solutions

customized storage solutions can be designed to fit the clients' specific needs and preferences. for example, a client who loves reading can have a custom bookshelf designed, while a client who loves fashion can have a custom closet with ample hanging space.

poor acoustic design

poor acoustic design is a mistake that can turn a serene retreat into a noisy and uncomfortable space. it happens when designers fail to consider the acoustic properties of the materials used in the design.

to fix this, consider the following:

  • use acoustic panels and sound-absorbing materials to reduce echo and reverberation
  • add area rugs and upholstered furniture to absorb sound
  • use soundproofing materials to block out external noise

incompatible materials

incompatible materials are a mistake that can lead to maintenance and durability issues in the long run. it happens when designers fail to consider the compatibility of the materials used in the design.
