bespoke interior design ideas for indian homes

Imagine walking into a stunning duplex in Greater Kailash, Delhi, where every element, from the intricate jali work to the plush furnishings, reflects your personal style. The space is a perfect blend of traditional Indian aesthetics and modern design sensibilities, creating a warm and inviting atmosphere that is quintessentially Indian. As you step inside, you notice the beautiful handcrafted wooden doors, the elegant chandelier, and the stunning artwork that adorns the walls, all of which come together to create a truly bespoke interior design that is tailored to your unique tastes and preferences.

traditional indian design

This design direction is perfect for those who want to incorporate traditional Indian elements into their home. The use of materials like wood, stone, and handmade ceramics can add a touch of warmth and authenticity to the space. For example, a beautifully crafted wooden mandir or a stunning stone jaali can be the focal point of the room. The cost range for this design direction can be around ₹10-12 lakh, depending on the materials and craftsmanship used. This design suits those who appreciate traditional Indian aesthetics and want to create a unique and culturally rich space.

key elements

  • handcrafted wooden furniture
  • intricately carved stone features
  • traditional indian textiles and patterns

modern minimalist design

This design direction is perfect for those who want to create a clean and modern space that is free from clutter and distractions. The use of materials like glass, steel, and concrete can add a touch of sophistication and elegance to the room. For example, a stunning glass chandelier or a sleek steel kitchen island can be the focal point of the room. The cost range for this design direction can be around ₹8-10 lakh, depending on the materials and fixtures used. This design suits those who appreciate modern design sensibilities and want to create a calm and serene space.

as indéva studio, we believe that modern minimalist design is not just about creating a blank canvas, but about carefully curating each element to create a space that is both beautiful and functional.

fusion design

This design direction is perfect for those who want to combine traditional Indian elements with modern design sensibilities. The use of materials like reclaimed wood, vintage fabrics, and handmade ceramics can add a touch of warmth and personality to the space. For example, a beautifully crafted wooden coffee table or a stunning handmade ceramic vase can be the focal point of the room. The cost range for this design direction can be around ₹12-15 lakh, depending on the materials and craftsmanship used. This design suits those who appreciate the beauty of fusion and want to create a unique and eclectic space.

tips for achieving fusion design

to achieve fusion design, it's essential to strike a balance between traditional and modern elements. this can be done by pairing traditional indian patterns and motifs with modern materials and fixtures. for example, you can pair a traditional indian print fabric with a modern steel chair or a vintage wooden door with a sleek glass chandelier.

sustainable design

This design direction is perfect for those who want to create a space that is not only beautiful but also environmentally friendly. The use of materials like recycled wood, bamboo, and low-voc paints can add a touch of sustainability to the space. For example, a stunning bamboo flooring or a beautiful recycled wood wall feature can be the focal point of the room. The cost range for this design direction can be around ₹10-12 lakh, depending on the materials and fixtures used. This design suits those who appreciate the importance of sustainability and want to create a space that is both beautiful and eco-friendly.
