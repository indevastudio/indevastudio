the surprising truth about interior design costs in india

when it comes to interior design, many of us assume that the cost will be negligible compared to the overall construction or purchase price of a property. however, the reality is that interior design can account for up to 20-30% of the total cost. in the case of a builder floor in sector 44, noida, with a budget of ₹40-80 lakh, the interior design cost can range from ₹8-24 lakh.

what drives costs up in interior design

several factors contribute to the overall cost of interior design, including the size of the space, materials used, and the level of customization. for example, a high-end villa in gurgaon with intricate designs and premium materials can easily exceed the budget of ₹80 lakh. on the other hand, a more modest design with mid-range materials can be achieved within the budget of ₹40-60 lakh.

cost breakdown by room

to get a better understanding of the costs involved, let's break down the estimated costs for each room in a typical villa:

  • living room: ₹1.5-3 lakh (furniture, lighting, and decor)
  • kitchen: ₹2-5 lakh (cabinets, countertops, and appliances)
  • bedrooms: ₹1-2 lakh per room (furniture, lighting, and decor)
  • bathrooms: ₹0.5-1.5 lakh per room (fixtures, tiles, and accessories)

additional costs to consider

in addition to the room-specific costs, there are other expenses to consider, such as:

  • electrical and plumbing work: ₹0.5-1 lakh
  • painting and wallpaper: ₹0.2-0.5 lakh
  • flooring: ₹0.5-1.5 lakh

how to negotiate with your interior designer

when working with an interior designer, it's essential to communicate your budget and expectations clearly. here are some tips to help you negotiate:

be transparent about your budget and priorities. if you have a specific design style or material in mind, let your designer know. they can help you find alternatives that fit within your budget.

red flags that inflate bills

there are several red flags to watch out for when working with an interior designer to avoid inflated bills:

  • unclear or vague contracts
  • lack of transparency in pricing and materials
  • unrealistic timelines and expectations
