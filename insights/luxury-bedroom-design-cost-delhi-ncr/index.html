the surprising truth about luxury bedroom design costs in india

when it comes to luxury bedroom design, many of us assume that the biggest expense will be the furniture and fixtures, but the reality is that labor costs and materials can add up quickly, making up as much as 60% of the total cost, which can range from ₹40 lakh to ₹80 lakh for a villa in Golf Course Road, Gurgaon.

what drives costs up in luxury bedroom design

several factors can drive up the cost of a luxury bedroom design project, including the size of the room, the materials used, and the level of customization, with high-end materials like teak wood and italian marble costing upwards of ₹5,000 per square foot, and custom furniture pieces costing ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh or more.

cost breakdown by room

a typical luxury bedroom design project can be broken down into several components, including:

  • flooring: ₹2 lakh to ₹5 lakh
  • walls and ceilings: ₹1.5 lakh to ₹3 lakh
  • furniture and fixtures: ₹5 lakh to ₹10 lakh
  • lighting and electrical: ₹1 lakh to ₹2 lakh
  • painting and finishing: ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh

getting the most out of your budget

to get the most out of your budget, it's essential to work with a experienced designer who can help you prioritize your spending and make the most of your space, as noted by elle decor india, "a well-designed bedroom can increase the value of your property and enhance your overall quality of life."

how to negotiate with your designer and contractor

when negotiating with your designer and contractor, it's essential to be clear about your budget and what you want to achieve, as this will help them provide you with a more accurate estimate and ensure that you get the most out of your money, for example, at indéva studio, we offer our services tailored to your specific needs and budget.

red flags that inflate bills

there are several red flags to watch out for when it comes to luxury bedroom design, including:

  • unrealistic timelines: rushing a project can lead to costly mistakes and delays
  • low-quality materials: using cheap materials can compromise the overall look and feel of the space
  • poor communication: failing to communicate clearly with your designer and contractor can lead to misunderstandings and cost overruns
