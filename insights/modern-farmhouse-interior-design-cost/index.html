modern farmhouse interior design cost india: a warning guide

the day a client called us mid-project, frantic about the skyrocketing costs of their heritage bungalow renovation in lutyens delhi, delhi, is a day that still haunts us. their ₹2 crore budget had ballooned to ₹4 crore, and they were at a loss for what to do. as a senior designer at indéva studio, i've seen this story play out time and time again - a well-intentioned homeowner gets caught up in the excitement of designing their dream home, only to find themselves drowning in a sea of unexpected expenses.

mistake 1: inadequate planning and budgeting

what goes wrong: homeowners often underestimate the true cost of renovating a heritage bungalow, failing to account for hidden expenses like structural repairs and custom finishes. why it happens: the excitement of designing a new space can lead to a lack of focus on the financial realities of the project. exact fix: work with a experienced designer to create a comprehensive budget and project plan, including a 10-20% contingency fund for unexpected expenses.

the importance of prioritization

  • identify your must-haves and nice-to-haves to help allocate budget and resources
  • consider the long-term costs of your design choices, including maintenance and upkeep
  • don't be afraid to compromise on non-essential elements to stay within budget

mistake 2: poor material selection

what goes wrong: choosing materials that are not durable or suited to the indian climate can lead to costly repairs and replacements down the line. why it happens: homeowners may be swayed by trendy or inexpensive options without considering the long-term implications. exact fix: work with a designer to select materials that balance aesthetics, functionality, and durability, such as sustainable wood options and low-maintenance finishes.

as a designer, it's my job to guide clients towards materials that will stand the test of time, not just look good in a magazine. for example, a client may love the look of a particular type of stone, but if it's not suitable for the indian climate, it may end up cracking or discoloring over time.

mistake 3: insufficient lighting design

what goes wrong: inadequate lighting can make a space feel dull and uninviting, and can even affect the overall ambiance of the home. why it happens: lighting is often an afterthought in the design process, with homeowners focusing on more visible elements like furniture and finishes. exact fix: work with a designer to create a comprehensive lighting plan that incorporates a mix of task, ambient, and accent lighting to create a warm and inviting atmosphere.

mistake 4: lack of expertise

what goes wrong: attempting to design and renovate a heritage bungalow without the guidance of a experienced professional can lead to costly mistakes and delays. why it happens: homeowners may try to DIY or work with inexperienced contractors to save money, but end up paying more in the long run. exact fix: hire a reputable and experienced designer and contractor to guide the project from start to finish, and consider contact us for a free consultation to get started.

mistake 5: not considering the bigger picture

what goes wrong: failing to consider the overall aesthetic and flow of the home can result in a disjointed and uninviting space. why it happens: homeowners may focus too much on individual rooms or elements, rather than considering how they fit into the larger design scheme. exact fix: work with a designer to create a cohesive design plan that takes into account the unique character of the heritage bungalow and the surrounding neighborhood, such as lutyens delhi, delhi.
