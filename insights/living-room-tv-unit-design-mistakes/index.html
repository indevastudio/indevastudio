living room tv unit design ideas india: mistakes to avoid

the day a client called us mid-project, frantic about their living room tv unit design, was a stark reminder of the importance of careful planning. the client had opted for a sleek, modern tv unit that clashed with the heritage bungalow's classic architecture in Lutyens Delhi, Delhi, and was now facing a ₹30 lakh redesign bill.

insufficient measurement and planning

what goes wrong: a tv unit that is too large or too small for the room, leading to an unbalanced and awkward layout. why it happens: inadequate measurement and planning, often due to a lack of experience or rushing through the design process. exact fix: take precise measurements of the room and the tv, and create a detailed design plan, considering factors like door swing, window placement, and furniture layout.

incompatible materials and finishes

what goes wrong: a tv unit that clashes with the surrounding decor, creating a jarring and unpleasant atmosphere. why it happens: poor material and finish selection, often due to a lack of understanding of the room's style and aesthetic. exact fix: choose materials and finishes that complement the room's existing decor, such as wood tones, metal accents, and fabric textures, and consider factors like durability and maintenance.

considering the room's style and aesthetic

  • traditional: opt for classic materials like wood and leather
  • modern: choose sleek materials like glass and metal
  • transitional: blend traditional and modern elements for a balanced look
the key to a successful tv unit design is to strike a balance between form and function, creating a piece that is both visually appealing and functional, as emphasized by the national institute of design.

ignoring the importance of lighting

what goes wrong: a tv unit that is poorly lit, creating harsh shadows and glare. why it happens: inadequate lighting planning, often due to a lack of consideration for the room's lighting needs. exact fix: incorporate lighting into the tv unit design, using a combination of overhead lighting, task lighting, and ambient lighting to create a warm and inviting atmosphere.

not considering the room's acoustics

what goes wrong: a tv unit that disrupts the room's acoustics, leading to echoey and unpleasant sound quality. why it happens: poor placement and design of the tv unit, often due to a lack of understanding of the room's acoustic needs. exact fix: consider the room's acoustics when designing the tv unit, using materials and placement that minimize echo and sound reflection, and opt for a design that incorporates sound-absorbing materials.

frequently asked questions

what is the average cost of a living room tv unit design in india?

the average cost of a living room tv unit design in india can range from ₹5 lakh to ₹50 lakh, depending on the materials, size, and complexity of the design.

how long does it take to design and install a living room tv unit?

the design and installation process for a living room tv unit can take anywhere from 2-6 weeks, depending on the complexity of the design and the availability of materials.

can i design my own living room tv unit?

while it is possible to design your own living room tv unit, it is often recommended to work with a professional designer to ensure a well-planned and functional design that meets your needs and budget.

what is the best material for a living room tv unit in a heritage bungalow?

the best material for a living room tv unit in a heritage bungalow in Lutyens Delhi, Delhi, would be a material that complements the existing decor, such as wood or metal, and is durable and easy to maintain.
