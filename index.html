about indé

we don't have
a legacy.
we have opinions.

indéva studio was founded to challenge how interiors are commonly designed and delivered. too many projects are built on surface aesthetics and unrealistic promises. we operate with conviction, clarity, and responsibility — from brief to handover, without exception.

founder
arpit saini
founder & ceo, indéva studio

indéva studio was founded to challenge the way interiors are commonly designed and delivered. too often, projects are driven by surface aesthetics, unrealistic promises, and a lack of accountability once execution begins. i wanted to build a firm that operates with honesty, clarity, and responsibility.

at indé, every decision is made with execution in mind — from space planning and furniture to materials and detailing. we speak openly about budgets, timelines, and constraints, and we stay involved until the work is finished as intended.

"we don't chase trends or design for applause. we believe good design should perform well in real life and age with time. that requires conviction, difficult conversations, and accountability from start to finish."

our philosophy

five pillars

pillar 01
human-centricity first

design is a service to the soul, not just an aesthetic exercise. we prioritize habitability before selecting a colour palette. we map morning light, the flow of conversation, and the physical comfort of a seated guest. a chair is only beautiful if it invites you to stay.

pillar 02
the invisible detail

true luxury is felt, not seen. we obsess over elements you don't notice until they're missing — the silent glide of a drawer, the tactile weight of a door handle, the acoustic warmth of a well-layered room. invisible engineering is the foundation that lets the aesthetic feel effortless.

pillar 03
the art of the mix

we reject the total look of a single era or brand. we pair sleek modern industrialism with the warmth of an antique. this juxtaposition creates a dialogue that prevents a space from feeling dated and allows it to remain timeless through its eclectic soul.

pillar 04
imperfection as character

a room that is too perfect is a room without character. we leave space for the happy accident — books slightly off-centre, a weathered patina, a rug with a softened edge. this quiet imperfection invites people to relax, not retreat.

pillar 05
confidence over rules

design rules are references, not commandments. we break them when a space asks us to — mixing patterns, shifting scale, placing art where it feels right. this confidence ensures the result is not a copy of a magazine, but an original expression of identity.

what defines us

our way of thinking

01
conviction over consensus

decisions are led, not diluted. we question briefs and challenge bad ideas early. design leadership means having opinions.

02
execution as design

what gets built is the only truth. detailing and drawings are developed for buildability, not presentation.

03
honest conversations early

we discuss budgets, risks, and constraints upfront. we don't sell unrealistic timelines or outcomes. late surprises are expensive.

04
architecture leads

interiors are shaped by spatial logic, not surface styling. if the plan is weak, nothing can save it.
